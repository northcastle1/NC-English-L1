The Secret Sauce: Vowels

Vowels are the "glue" that holds words together. In Street English, they change everything based on their "length".

Short Sounds

Quick and punchy.

"Cat", "Bed", "Sit"

Long Sounds

The letter says its own name.

"Cake", "Feet", "Ice"

The Common Mistake: P vs B

Many beginners mix these up. The secret is the Tissue Test:

  • P Explosive: Hold a tissue in front of your mouth. When you say "Peter", the tissue must move.
  • B Vocal: When you say "Baby", your throat vibrates, but no air comes out.
