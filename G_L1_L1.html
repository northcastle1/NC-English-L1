Module 1: Lecture 1 - Alphabet & Street Sounds

Letter Names vs. Street Sounds

In school, they taught you the Alphabet Names (A, B, C). In the street, we use Street Sounds. A letter's name is just its ID card, but its sound is how it actually speaks to you.

"The letter 'A' is named 'Ay', but in the street, it says 'Ah', 'Aa', or 'Uh'!"

Street Secrets

The P vs B Battle

Put your hand in front of your mouth. When you say 'P', you should feel a burst of air. When you say 'B', your throat vibrates but there is no wind! Don't call your Barking dog a Parking dog!

Short vs Long Vowels

Short vowels are fast and punchy (Bit, Cut). Long vowels say their own name (Bite, Cute). In the street, getting these wrong changes the whole meaning of your story!
