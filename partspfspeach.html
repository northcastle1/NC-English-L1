Grammar: Parts of Speech

Understanding parts of speech is essential for determining the correct definition of a word when using the dictionary.

There are eight parts of speech in English: nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, prepositions, conjunctions, and interjections.

1. Noun

A noun is the name of a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns can be singular or plural.

Examples: John, Cairo, Happiness

  • John lives in Cairo.
  • The girl is beautiful.

2. Pronoun

A pronoun is a word used in place of a noun.

Examples: She, we, they, it, He

  • John lives in Cairo. He is 12 years old.
  • The girl is beautiful. She has long hair.

3. Verb

A verb expresses action or being.

Examples: play, jump, write, is

  • John lives in Cairo.
  • The girl is beautiful.
  • He wrote a letter.

4. Adjective

Modifies or describes a noun or pronoun.

Examples: pretty, old, big, smart

"He is a smart boy."

5. Adverb

Modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

Examples: quickly, slowly, very

"He walked slowly."

6. Preposition

Tells relationships between words.

Examples: in, on, at, by, with

"John lives in Cairo."

7. Conjunction

Joins words, phrases, or clauses.

Examples: and, but, or, while, because

"Sara and Nancy are good friends."

8. Interjection

A word used to express emotion. Often followed by an exclamation point!

Wow! - Oh! - Oops!

"Wow! I like your dress."
