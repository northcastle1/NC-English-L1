Parts of Speech

Understanding parts of speech is essential for determining the correct definition of a word when using the dictionary. There are eight parts of speech in English.

The Eight Pillars of English

Noun

Name of a person, place, thing, or idea.
Ex: John, Cairo, Happiness

Pronoun

Used in place of a noun.
Ex: She, we, they, it, He

Verb

Expresses action or being.
Ex: play, jump, write, is

Adjective

Modifies/describes a noun or pronoun.
Ex: pretty, old, smart

Adverb

Modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb.
Ex: quickly, slowly, very

Preposition

Shows relationships between words.
Ex: in, on, at, by

Conjunction

Joins words, phrases, or clauses.
Ex: and, but, or, because

Interjection

Expresses emotion (Wow!, Oh!).
Often uses an exclamation point.

Applied Grammar

Usage in Context

  • John (Noun) lives in (Preposition) Cairo (Noun).
  • He (Pronoun) walked slowly (Adverb).
  • Wow! (Interjection) I like your long (Adjective) hair.
