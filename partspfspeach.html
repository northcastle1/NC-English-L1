Parts of Speech

Understanding parts of speech is essential for determining the correct definition of a word when using the dictionary[cite: 5]. Master the eight building blocks of English: nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, prepositions, conjunctions, and interjections[cite: 6].

01. The Noun [cite: 7]

A noun is the name of a person, place, thing, or idea. They can be singular or plural[cite: 8, 9, 10].

Examples[cite: 11, 13, 14]:

  • John lives in Cairo.
  • The girl is beautiful.
  • Happiness is a state of mind.

02. The Pronoun [cite: 15]

A word used in place of a noun. Common pronouns include: She, we, they, it, He[cite: 16, 17].

In Action[cite: 19, 20]:

"John lives in Cairo. He is 12 years old."

"The girl is beautiful. She has long hair."

03. The Verb [cite: 21]

A verb expresses action or being[cite: 22]. Without verbs, sentences have no life.

play [cite: 23]
jump [cite: 23]
write [cite: 23]
is [cite: 23]

Ex: He wrote a letter[cite: 25].

Modifiers & Details

Adjectives [cite: 26]

Modifies or describes a noun or pronoun[cite: 27].

"I like long hair." [cite: 29]

Adverbs [cite: 32]

Modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb[cite: 33].

"He walked slowly." [cite: 35]

Structural Elements

Prepositions [cite: 37]

Tell relationship between words[cite: 38]. Ex: in, on, at, by[cite: 39].

Conjunctions [cite: 45]

Joins words, phrases, or clauses[cite: 46]. Ex: and, but, or, because[cite: 48].

Interjections [cite: 51]

Expresses emotion! Often followed by an exclamation point[cite: 52, 53].

Wow! [cite: 54] Oh! [cite: 54] Oops! [cite: 54]

"Wow! I like your dress." [cite: 55]
