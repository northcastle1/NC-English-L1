Lecture 1: The Alphabet & Street Sounds

The Alphabet
vs. The Street

In the classroom, you learn the "Names" of letters. But on the street, people don't talk in names; they talk in "Sounds".

Think of it like a person: Their name is 'A', but their voice is '/æ/'. To master Street English, we must stop reading and start listening.

Listen

Focus on the vibration and air.

Speak

Copy the "Street" rhythm.

The Sound Gallery

A
Street: /æ/

Open your mouth wide like you're biting an apple. Flat tongue.

B
Street: /b/

An explosive sound. Press lips together and release with a voice vibration.

K
Street: /k/

Push a sharp puff of air from the back of your throat. No voice!

S
Street: /s/

Hiss like a snake. Air passes through your teeth. No throat vibration.

The Vowel Optical Illusion

Vowels are the "engines" of words. They change everything.

Short Vowels

Quick and clipped sounds. Found in words like Cat, Bed, Sit, Hot, Sun.

Long Vowels

The vowel says its own name. Found in words like Cake, Keep, Bike, Home, Cute.
