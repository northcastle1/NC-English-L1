Lecture 1: Secrets of Sounds (Alphabet & Street Sounds)

The Core Rule

English is a language of SOUNDS, not Letters.

In many languages, you say what you see. In English, letters are just "costumes." To speak like a local, you must train your ears to hear the vibrations, not just read the alphabet.

The Sound Factory

Your mouth is a machine. Different sounds come from different "stations":

  • Throat Sounds (G, K): Feel the vibration at the back of your tongue.
  • Lip Sounds (P, B): Use air pressure to "pop" the sound.

Pro Tip: Stand in front of a mirror. If your lips don't move for 'P' and 'B', you aren't saying them right!

Vibration Check

Place two fingers on your throat. Say "BZZZZ". Feel that? That is a Voiced sound. Now say "SSSSS". No vibration? That is Unvoiced.
